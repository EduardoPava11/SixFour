# SixFour V2 Corpus ‚Üí Haskell Tooling: The Granular Mapping

The zero-dependency contract dominates this whole map. The shipped Tier-2 iOS product has no third-party deps, and the Tier-0 spec is GHC-boot-only (base, vector, containers, text, transformers). So for almost every concept the verdict is the same shape: the library is a dev-tooling oracle at best, and the real prize is the API/typeclass-design lesson we hand-write into the boot-only spec. Two libraries (`GHC.TypeLits`, `Data.Type.Equality`) are the rare ALREADY-BOOT exceptions, and they are already in use.

## 2. Strongest Reuse-In-Tooling Candidates (dev exes only, never the shipped spec)

These touch dev-tooling executables (spec-tui, test harnesses, codegen golden-generators) and never link into Tier-2 or the boot-only spec:

- **`arithmoi` as a number-theory golden cross-check (highest value).** A QuickCheck property suite that asserts our hand-written ‚Ñ§[œâ] and ‚Ñ§[i] `norm`, `units`, `conjugate`, and ramified-(1‚àíœâ) valuation agree with `EisensteinIntegers.norm`/`ids`/`associates` and `GaussianIntegers` (note: arithmoi has NO ramified-divide function, so the (1‚àíœâ) valuation has no oracle, it is checked against our own algebra). This is a throwaway oracle that catches sign/basis bugs in the hand-rolled rings. It is the single most justified library use on the whole map.
- **`HaskellForMaths` `Math.Projects.RootSystem` as a Gram-matrix golden generator.** Run `form A 7`, `form A 2`, `cartanMatrix` once in a codegen/tooling exe to emit the fixed 7√ó7 and 2√ó2 integer Gram matrices and the det-6 form, then hand-encode those small constants into the spec and keep the HaskellForMaths output as the golden the spec test compares against.
- **`cyclotomic` as a one-time exact-constant computer.** Compute the exact Œ∂‚ÇÜ/Œ∂‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇ rotation coefficients over a fixed ‚Ñ§-basis once, bake the resulting integer/dyadic constants into the spec. The compositum ‚Ñö(Œ∂‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇ) is the smallest field holding both i and œâ = the algebraic home of "keep both lenses," but its sparse-Map engine is overkill to ship.
- **`hgeometry` as a geometry diagnostic exe.** Render the A‚ÇÇ Voronoi/Delaunay tiling to visually confirm `closestLambda`'s decision regions are the correct hexagonal cells; bound the reachable chroma gamut with its convex hull. Pure visual sanity-check, never on the byte-exact path (its `r ~ Double` default is exactly the non-exactness we forbid).
- **`grid` `Hexagonal` / `diagrams-contrib` Tilings** as an A‚ÇÇ neighbour-structure validator and hex-lattice visualizer for the spec-tui.
- **`type-natural` / `subhask`** as proof-carrying / law-attachment references in tests only (machine-checked doubling-composition lemmas, per-class QuickCheck laws).

## 3. API/Typeclass Designs Worth Stealing (reimplement dependency-free)

The three highest-value steals, then the rest:

1. **arithmoi's `Euclidean` class sitting strictly below `Field`** (with `norm`, `quotRem`, `extendedGCD`, and units-as-a-finite-list `ids`). Hand-write a `Euclidean` class that has `quotRem` and deliberately **stops short of `recip`**, this type-enforces our entire "byte-exactness forbids dividing by non-units ‚Üí module theory not linear algebra" thesis at the class level. ‚Ñ§[1/2], ‚Ñ§[œâ], ‚Ñ§[i] all live at "Euclidean but not Field." (Note: arithmoi has NO ramified-prime extractor; the `(multiplicity, reduced)` shape for our (1‚àíœâ) floor-seam is our own design, no library precedent.)

2. **`linear`'s squared-only metric split** (`quadrance`/`qd`/`dot` are `Num`-only and exact; `norm`/`distance`/`signorm` are `Floating`). Our `RModule` metric must expose only the squared, integer-valued `quadrance`/`qd` and a `dot`-against-a-Gram-matrix, and NEVER add a `norm` method that forces `Floating`. `Metric` sits atop `Additive`, mirroring `RModule` over `CommutativeRing`.

3. **Kmett `algebra`'s `Module` layering + the free-module-as-`basis->coeff` representation.** The scalar superclass is `Semiring`/`Rng`, never `Field`, module-not-vector-space made into a typeclass. Layering `Additive ‚Üí Group ‚Üí Rng/Semiring ‚Üí Module`. Representing a free module as a Representable map `basis -> coeff` fuses `RModule` with the S‚ÇÇ‚ÇÖ‚ÇÜ `GaugeAction` (the gauge = precomposition with a basis permutation). `vector-space`'s explicit `AdditiveGroup`-vs-`VectorSpace` split is the same boundary made concrete: take only `AdditiveGroup` + ring-scaling, stop before field-scaling.

Supporting steals:
- **arithmoi's `(:+)` constructor shared by Eisenstein and Gaussian** ‚áí one `CommutativeRing`/`Euclidean` typeclass with two instances, not two bespoke types. `norm :: a -> Integer` as a multiplicative ring-hom to ‚Ñï is your `lawNormMultiplicative`.
- **arithmoi's `Mod (m::Nat)` + `MultMod` smart constructor** ‚áí carry the modulus/scale at the type level (`Scale (n::Nat)` phantom prevents cross-scale band mixing); gate the gauge through a units-only smart constructor.
- **`Data.Functor.Rep`/Naperian `tabulate`/`index`/`positions`** ‚áí state the SoA round-trip as the Representable law, get `liftR2` (byte-exact elementwise Haar) and `Log(Compose)=product` (principled `16*16==256` via composition, and the 16‚Üí256 self-similar refinement as iterated functor composition) for free.
- **HaskellForMaths' "one `form` matrix, not a `QuadraticForm` typeclass"** ‚áí you do NOT need a quadratic-form typeclass; you need one `gram :: [[Integer]]` value + `bilinear u v = u¬∑gram¬∑v`.
- **`lambda-ski`'s bracket-abstraction + optimization rules** ‚áí canonicalize the residual SKI word before reduction; **`graph-rewriting-ski`'s explicit-sharing graph rewriting** ‚áí the honest "reduce the word to build 256¬≥ without materializing it" cost model.
- **`recursion-schemes`' base-functor `hylo`** ‚áí `data OctF a = Leaf RGB | Node a a a a a a a a`, then `hylo alg coalg` fuses octree build+collapse with no intermediate 256¬≥ allocated; the reversible lift = an `(Algebra, Coalgebra)` mutual-inverse pair.
- **`galois-field`'s explicit `Field`(Data.Euclidean)+`Group`(Data.Group) superclass edge** ‚áí confirms our layering (Group for the gauge, Euclidean-NOT-Field for the carrier); copy the edge but keep carriers on the Euclidean side.

## 4. Where Haskell Has Nothing Good (we are on our own)

Honest gaps where reimplementing is the only option, not a convenience:

- **Byte-exact reversible-integer Haar / lifting scheme.** No Hackage lifting-scheme library, and certainly none that is integer-exact and invertible. The `(Algebra, Coalgebra)` mutual-inverse framing is ours to write. This is the core codec primitive and it is entirely DIY.
- **The index-6 closest-point (`closestLambda`) / Conway-Sloane root-lattice decoders.** Nobody packages the fast O(n) A_n/D_n/E‚Çà/Œõ decoders from SPLAG Ch. 20. `Lattices.CloseVector` is generic rational Babai (wrong arithmetic, wrong algorithm); `hgeometry` KD-tree is nearest-*sample* not nearest-*lattice*. Already hand-rolled in the corpus with a proven `cost ‚â§ 1`; the absence of any library confirms this is a genuine own-primitive, not a gap to fill.
- **Exact-integer quadratic / symmetric-bilinear forms.** No Hackage package for Gram-matrix classification, det/signature, or form equivalence over ‚Ñ§. `hmatrix`/`lapack` are floating LAPACK only. The det-6 opponent form and energy-weighted metric have no off-the-shelf home (HaskellForMaths `form` only generates the root-system Gram as a golden, it doesn't classify).
- **Shannon entropy / mutual information / KL in Haskell.** None exists (the `entropy` package is crypto RNG; `infotheory`/`infotheo` are C++/Python/R). Histogram entropy is `group . sort` + a log, already boot-only in the corpus.
- **Reversible/injective combinatory logic.** All SKI libraries model non-injective S/K (K erases an argument = irreversible). Our reversible byte-exact word (Eisenstein units, luma shifts, swaps) has no library; only the B-composition stack shape transfers.
- **3D octree / 3-way Morton interleave.** `persistent-spatial` is strictly 2D and DB-coupled. The 3-bit-digit octree interleave (the A‚Çá 1+7 split) is hand-rolled over boot `Data.Bits`.
- **The SKI-as-CNN-wiring / PonderNet-halt-as-read-depth bridge.** No library connects combinatory reduction depth to a learned halting/read-depth signal; this is bespoke architecture, not a packaged algorithm.

## 5. Name-Clash and False-Friend Warnings

- **`lattices` (lowercase) and `semilattices` = ORDER THEORY, not geometry.** `JoinSemiLattice`/`MeetSemiLattice`/`BoundedLattice`, join `\/`=sup, meet `/\`=inf, `Set`/`IntSet` instances, Kleene `lfp`. It has no `norm`, no closest-vector, no basis, no Gram matrix. Do NOT `cabal add lattices` for A‚Çá/A‚ÇÇ/‚Ñ§[œâ]/Œõ work. The only faint legitimate overlap: the ultrametric scale filtration (16‚äë32‚äë‚Ä¶‚äë256) and octree prefix order ARE a real order-theoretic poset, so `BoundedLattice` could model "coarsest ‚äë finest" in tooling, but never for the chroma/root lattices.
- **`Lattices` (capital L, bcoppens) = geometric, but tiny/Rational/educational.** LLL + Babai closest-vector over `Rational`, its own README admits "no optimizations." Right meaning, wrong arithmetic (we are exact-integer) and wrong algorithm (root lattices need no LLL). The capital/lowercase collision itself is worth a one-line spec note.
- **`galois-field` / `finite-field` / `finite-fields` = GF(p^q), a rejected-jargon trap.** "Field" matches our chroma "field," but GF(256) imposes an irreducible-polynomial multiplication our **permutation gauge S‚ÇÇ‚ÇÖ‚ÇÜ** and our **non-field carriers (‚Ñ§[1/2], ‚Ñ§[œâ])** both reject. The palette is invariant theory / S‚ÇÇ‚ÇÖ‚ÇÜ orbits, NOT a field. (Steal only the `char`/`deg`/`frob` interface idea and the `Field`+`Group` superclass edge.)
- **`free-vector-spaces` "free" = free over a FIELD,** not a free ‚Ñ§-module. Wrong tool for P6 = ‚Ñ§‚Å∂; use the Comonad.Reader free-module (`basis -> coeff`) construction instead.
- **`linear` and `vector-space` are field-biased despite "free vector space" language.** Their scalar-mult laws and higher modules (`Data.Maclaurin`, `Data.LinearMap`) assume `Fractional`/divisibility. Use them ONLY for the `Additive`/`V_n` array shape and the `quadrance`/`qd` split; take the module *laws* from Kmett `algebra`'s `Semiring`-scalar `Module`.
- **Hackage `entropy` = cryptographic randomness (RDRAND/urandom), NOT information theory.** No Shannon/MI/KL package exists in Haskell.
- **`cl3` / `clifford` / `clif` (geometric algebra) are `Double`-valued.** The name matches ("geometric algebra," and a hue rotation IS a rotor in the chroma bivector plane), but the float base disqualifies them from the byte-exact path. Steal only the grade-tagged-constructors pattern (mirrors our L/a/b/x/y/t and P6 axis tagging); never the package, and never their `Ord`-by-singular-values.
- **`representable-functors` is deprecated** in favor of `adjunctions`'s `Data.Functor.Rep`; cite the latter.
