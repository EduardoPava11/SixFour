## Hard constraints on the canonical core (per this axis)
- DEPLOYED NET MUST BE A FIXED-POINT INTEGER GRAPH owned by the Zig Q16 core (8/16-bit weights, 16-bit activations, 32-bit accumulators with @divFloor overflow traps, monotonic activations via bit-shift/LUT not transcendentals), trained as float in MLX then quantize-frozen; golden-vectored Zig==Swift==Haskell. Precedent: arXiv:2312.11209 (MSE=0 cross-platform), Jacob et al. integer-only inference.
- PLANNING = GUMBEL-ALPHAZERO over the REAL reversible LAB-collapse simulator (R/P operators), NOT MuZero: the move machine is a perfect deterministic+reversible simulator, so a learned dynamics model is unnecessary and is the empirically fragile low-simulation configuration. Adopt Gumbel-Top-k + Sequential Halving + completed-Q policy target; integer Q16 argmax key over g+logits+sigma(q).
- NET IS A TINY POLICY+VALUE PREDICTOR (target <130K params): 3-residual-block (MiniZero precedent) or pooled-set/Deep-Sets encoder at small width over the 384-DOF generator space. The net only needs to be good enough that few-simulation (n=8-16 recommended; n=2 works but trains slowly/game-dependently) search PROVABLY improves on it.
- REWARD = BRADLEY-TERRY LATENT STRENGTH as the value-head target (BT preference == entropy-regularized RL value); the per-device 770-D theta and sigma-locked LeafOverride enter as IN-CONTEXT / amortized conditioning at INFERENCE on the frozen integer graph, never as weight retraining. Preserves bit-agreement under personalization.
- EQUIVARIANCE IS STRUCTURAL, NOT LEARNED: operate the net in sigma-pair generator space where the 256-leaf sigma-symmetry is an EXACT integer operator; do not spend parameters approximating permutation equivariance. Use at most a single inducing-point/ISAB or Deep-Sets pool, not a deep set transformer.
- KEEP DETERMINISTIC MAXIMIN COLLAPSE (P operator, already shipping GIFA/GIFB) as the base; do NOT replace it with a neural-OT barycenter (adversarial bi-level, continuous-support, wrong for discrete palettes) or discrete diffusion (multi-step float, non-portable). Any learned OT enrichment must be a FIXED small unrolled-Sinkhorn integer layer feeding the value head, additive to the deterministic base, consistent with the JEPA-vs-statistical 'deterministic base + thin learned residual' verdict.

## Findings
### [high] Gumbel AlphaZero replaces AlphaZero's heuristic root mechanisms (Dirichlet noise + PUCB visit-count target) with Gumbel-Top-k sampling without replacement + Sequential Halving, and GUARANTEES policy improvement after as few as n=2 simulations provided visited actions' Q-values are correctly evaluated. It has no problem-dependent hyperparameters (Sequential Halving) and the paper proves E[q(A_{n+1})] >= sum_a pi(a) q(a) (Eq. 6-7).
**Implication:** This is the canonical core's planning algorithm. The reversible LAB-collapse state machine (R operator forward, maximin P collapse) IS the perfect simulator, so you run real MCTS over it (no learned dynamics model needed). Adopt Gumbel-AlphaZero search verbatim: Gumbel-Top-k on policy logits at the root, Sequential Halving over m sampled actions, deterministic completed-Q policy target (Eq. 10-12) so the value head fills unvisited actions. The whole search is integer-friendly: sigma(q) is a monotonic transform (Eq. 8 uses c_visit=50, c_scale=1.0) and the argmax of g+logits+sigma(q) is exactly the kind of Q16 argmax-key comparison your SIMT bit-agreement contract already enforces.
**Sources:** https://davidstarsilver.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/gumbel-alphazero.pdf, https://openreview.net/forum?id=bERaNdoegnO

### [high] In MiniZero's controlled study, the board-game net is only '3 residual blocks' and Gumbel variants were trained at 2 and 16 simulations vs standard AlphaZero's 200. Adversarial caveat: at n=2 on 9x9 Go, Gumbel AlphaZero 'grows slowly but eventually reaches a similar performance' and Gumbel MuZero n=2 was the WORST of six models; but on 8x8 Othello n=2 was equal to or slightly better than n=16. So 'learns reliably at n=2' is true for AlphaZero-style (real simulator) but is game-dependent and slower early; the learned-model (MuZero) n=2 is fragile.
**Implication:** Two concrete constraints. (1) Use the AlphaZero branch (real reversible simulator), NOT MuZero: a learned dynamics net at low sim counts is the fragile configuration AND would add a second non-bit-exact float surface to port. Your perfect reversible move machine is exactly the case where MuZero's learned model is unnecessary. (2) A 3-block residual trunk at small width (e.g. 32-48 channels) lands well under 130K params, but budget for SLOW early training at very low sim counts; n=8-16 is a safer operating point than n=2 if training time matters. The net's job is a cheap policy+value predictor that makes few-sim search provably improve.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/html/2310.11305v3, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.11305

### [high] The Bradley-Terry model is the canonical preference-to-reward map; minimizing the BT preference loss on a soft-optimal Q-function is provably equivalent to recovering the optimal policy for entropy-regularized RL (preference optimization == entropy-regularized RL).
**Implication:** Your A/B Bradley-Terry preference is a mathematically legitimate scalar reward for the value head, not a hack. The value head should predict the BT latent strength of a terminal palette/look; the Gumbel-AlphaZero value target is then this BT-derived value. This grounds the existing btUpdate/theta=770-D taste vector: theta parameterizes a per-device BT reward, the SHARED net learns palette-quality structure, and the per-device theta tilts terminal value. This also explains why the supervised look-net failed (grayscale-L non-convergence): MSE-to-a-target is the wrong objective; BT-preference-as-value over a search is the right one, consistent with the in-repo 'NN win must be perceptual not MSE' prior.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.12501, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2508.11363

### [high] Cross-platform bit-exact neural inference is an established, published pattern: arXiv:2312.11209 quantizes an entire decoder to fixed-point (8/16-bit weights, 16-bit activations, 32-bit accumulators with overflow prevention) and replaces LeakyReLU with a bit-shift lookup-table, achieving MSE=0 between GPU and CPU reconstructions. Jacob et al. integer-arithmetic-only inference is the foundational method.
**Implication:** This is the direct precedent for the Zig Q16 core owning the deployed net (your memory already cites 2312.11209). Constraint on the canonical core: the on-device policy+value net must be a fixed-point integer graph (Q16 weights/activations, 32-bit accumulators, @divFloor-trapped, simd_sum reassociation pinned, monotonic integer activations via bit-shift/LUT rather than transcendental tanh/exp). Train float in MLX (Tier 1, Mac), then quantize-and-freeze to the Zig integer graph as the single source of truth, golden-vectored Zig==Swift==Haskell. The MLX float model is the trainer; the Zig integer model is the artifact that ships and must bit-agree.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/html/2312.11209, https://www.adrian.idv.hk/2022-12-02-jkczthak17-quantize/, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2102.02147

### [high] Set Transformer (ISAB/SAB equivariant blocks + PMA invariant pooling) is a universal approximator of permutation-invariant set functions, with inducing points reducing attention from quadratic to linear in set size.
**Implication:** The palette is a SET of leaves/generators, so the encoder should be permutation-equivariant by construction, not a fixed-order MLP. But adopt the CHEAP version: a Deep Sets / single-ISAB pooled-set encoder (the L-NN research already settled on a 'pooled set encoder'), not a deep multi-head transformer, to stay under 130K params and integer-portable. Critically, your sigma-pair genome (384-DOF / 128 generators reconstructing a 256-leaf sigma-symmetric palette) already encodes the symmetry as an EXACT integer operator: do NOT spend parameters learning equivariance the genome gives you for free. The net operates in generator space; equivariance is structural, matching the 'symmetry-as-exact-OKLab-op' design and the LeafOverride sigma-locked delta.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/1810.00825, http://proceedings.mlr.press/v97/lee19d/lee19d.pdf

### [medium] Amortized / in-context inference lets a pretrained network adapt to a new task in a single forward pass with NO parameter updates, by conditioning on context (set-based conditioning models, learned-optimizer / ICL unification).
**Implication:** This validates the LeafOverride / theta-taste design as the inference-time adaptation mechanism: rather than retraining on each user's A/B choices, condition the (frozen, bit-exact) policy on the 770-D theta vector and the sigma-locked generator-space delta. The net is trained ONCE (population BT preferences); per-device taste enters as in-context conditioning + the btUpdate-maintained theta, applied at inference without touching weights. This keeps the deployed integer graph frozen and golden-gated while still personalizing, which is the only way to preserve bit-agreement under per-device adaptation.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.11471v1, https://arxiv.org/html/2502.02463

### [high] Neural-OT Wasserstein-barycenter methods (e.g. arXiv:2402.03828) rely on bi-level ADVERSARIAL training and target continuous distributions / general cost via dual potentials; discrete free-support barycenters are classically solved by (regularized) Sinkhorn or k-means-style updates, and unrolled Sinkhorn's memory grows L(n+m) with iteration count L.
**Implication:** Do NOT replace the deterministic maximin collapse with a neural-OT barycenter net. Three reasons grounded in the stack: (1) palettes are DISCRETE finite-support measures and the in-repo Bures/sigma-pair note already established Gaussian-barycenter approximation is wrong for discrete palettes; (2) neural-OT barycenter solvers are adversarial bi-level, the opposite of a tiny bit-exact portable net; (3) Lloyd-Max is already the MSE ceiling per your priors. Keep maximin collapse (P operator) as the deterministic, already-shipping, golden-gated base. If you want a learned OT flavor, the only portable option is a FIXED small number of UNROLLED Sinkhorn iterations as an integer Q16 layer (bounded L => bounded memory, no fixed-point solver, no adversary) feeding the value head, NOT a free solver.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03828, https://arxiv.org/pdf/1310.4375, https://arxiv.org/pdf/1803.00567

### [medium] Discrete diffusion / simplex-flow generators over categorical data exist (Categorical SDEs, ShortListing 'progressive candidate pruning'), framing discrete generation as iterative pruning to a single choice.
**Implication:** Tempting but OFF-AXIS for the deployed net: discrete diffusion requires many sampling steps and float score networks, neither portable nor bit-exact, and it would REPLACE rather than build on the deterministic collapse + search. Note however the structural rhyme: ShortListing's 'progressively prune the full category set to one choice' is literally Sequential Halving in Gumbel-AlphaZero. So the right place for 'iterative pruning over palettes' is the MCTS search itself (which you already keep), not a separate diffusion model. Reject diffusion for deployment; it offers no win the search doesn't already give under the portability constraint.
**Sources:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2210.14784, https://arxiv.org/html/2508.17345v1

## Risks
- n=2 simulations is the headline Gumbel claim but is game-dependent and trains SLOWLY early (MiniZero: g-AlphaZero n=2 on 9x9 Go lags badly before catching up; g-MuZero n=2 was worst of six). Over-relying on n=2 to keep on-device search cheap could stall training; treat n=2 as a floor, operate near n=8-16.
- Quantize-after-train (float MLX -> integer Zig) can shift the argmax of g+logits+sigma(q) near ties, breaking the policy-improvement guarantee which assumes 'correctly evaluated' Q-values. Need quantization-aware training or a verified error bound, plus golden vectors that specifically probe near-tie action rankings, or bit-agreement passes while DECISIONS silently differ.
- Bradley-Terry value targets are NOISY (single A/B comparisons) and the soft-optimal-Q equivalence assumes Boltzmann-rational, transitive preferences; real per-device taste may be intransitive/non-stationary, biasing the value head. The 770-D theta could overfit a few swipes; needs a population prior (shared net) + regularized btUpdate.
- Equivariance-as-exact-op only holds if the net never breaks sigma-symmetry; if any layer mixes leaves across the sigma-pairing (e.g. a dense head over 256 leaves instead of 128 generators) the structural equivariance is lost and you pay for it in parameters and in golden-vector drift.
- 'Tiny net + real-simulator MCTS' shifts cost from parameters to per-move tree search at inference; even n=8 Gumbel search runs the integer net 8x plus collapse/Haar per node on-device. Latency, not params, may be the real on-device bottleneck; the fluidity-systems memory (main-thread contention) is the relevant prior risk.
- Unrolled-Sinkhorn-as-integer-layer is unproven at Q16: entropic OT involves exp/log that are exactly the transcendentals that break bit-exactness; a fixed-iteration integer Sinkhorn needs LUT-based exp and a proven error bound, otherwise it reintroduces the float non-determinism the whole core is designed to avoid.
