# SixFour V2.1, the pre-collapse probabilistic field

2026-06-29. V2.1 is **not** a new model direction away from V2; it is the layer **above V2's
encode boundary**. V2 stores a collapsed scalar latent `[L,a,b,x,y,t]` per voxel. V2.1 says: that
scalar is the *measurement* of an underlying **probability curve**, and the thing worth training on
is the curve, not its shadow. Everything V2 locked still holds, V2.1 only adds the layer that
produces the bytes V2's `encodeBoundary` already consumes.

Companion exploration module: `spec/exploration/V21Definitions.hs` (base-only, `runghc`, **9/9 green**
NOT wired into cabal/Map/gate, the standard exploration status).

## 1. The one-line thesis

**R, G, B, x, y, t are pure functions. R/G/B are probability curves (energy landscapes) over a
bin's value domain; x/y/t are coordinate functions with a metric that is linear in x,y and weighted
in t. The GIF89a the user sees is the energy-minimising *collapse* of the curves. We train on the
curves themselves; we *encode* the ±1 neighbour deltas of the curves.**

```
        R,G,B : Field -> Voxel -> Curve            -- Curve = Level -> Energy,  p(level) ~ exp(-E)
        x,y,t : Voxel -> Int                       -- coordinate functions
     collapse : Curve -> Level                     -- argmin energy  ==  the GIF89a byte
   collapse . curve  ==  the sRGB byte that V2Latent.encodeBoundary consumes
```

So V2.1 ⟶ (collapse) ⟶ V2. No locked decision is reopened (the latent is still opponent-literal;
the boundary discipline still holds; Eisenstein is still a derived lens; the /3 guard is unchanged).

---

## 2. Why "the bins are the probability functions"

A frame is **64×64 bins**. Each bin's payload is **three sampled energy curves** (R, G, B), each a
fixed-length vector of `nLevels` doubles (256 for byte-exact GIF89a). That fixed length is the whole
point of the SIMT constraint:

- No pointers, no variable-length payload, **no neighbour reads at creation time**.
- One GPU thread fills one bin. The 64×64 (×64 frames) bin creation is *embarrassingly parallel*.
- `lawBinIsSimtFlat` pins the contract: every channel vector is exactly `nLevels` long.

The "curve" is the pure-function view (`Curve = Level -> Double`); the "bin" is its SIMT
materialisation (`data Bin = Bin [Double] [Double] [Double]`). `sampleCurve`/`asCurve` move between
them. The model trains on `Bin`; the user sees `collapse`.

---

## 3. "Collapsed energy levels", the Boltzmann link

Each curve is written **energy-first**: `E(level)`, with `p(level) = exp(-E(level)) / Z`. Two facts
make "energy" and "probability" the same object (so "we still need energy" is free, the channel
*is* the energy):

- `collapse c = argmin_level E(level)` (`lawCollapseIsArgminEnergy`).
- `argmin E == argmax p == the mode` (`lawCollapseIsMode`), the Boltzmann map is order-reversing.

The GIF89a is therefore the **ground state** of the field: the per-bin energy minimum, read out as a
byte. Training on the curve = training on the whole energy landscape, not just its floor.

---

## 4. The encode target is the **delta**, not the curve

For neighbour voxels at **±1 in x and/or y and/or t** (the 6-neighbour von Neumann stencil
`neighbours`), we take the **per-channel curve delta** `ΔC(level) = C₁(level) − C₂(level)`. That delta
is what we encode. Flat regions ⟶ near-zero delta (cheap); edges ⟶ spend. This is the distributional
lift of the project's existing relational-residual principle.

**L,a,b are built from the deltas.** The opponent transform `(L,a,b) = (R+G+B, R−G, R+G−2B)` is
**linear**, so `opp(ΔR,ΔG,ΔB) == Δ(L,a,b)`, proven by `lawOpponentCommutesWithDelta`. This is the
exact distributional twin of `V2CrossFrameBEnergy`'s "t-detail band = ownerB(f1)−ownerB(f2), exact by
linearity." It is the licence to encode deltas and recover opponent colour without information loss.

---

## 5. The metric: x,y linear; t weighted at the per-frame palette delta

`axisWeight` is the single place the box's anisotropy lives:

| axis | weight | meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| X | `1` | spatial, linear |
| Y | `1` | spatial, linear, **linear to x** (shared isotropic spatial metric) |
| T | `PaletteDelta` | temporal, a step in t is charged at the **per-frame palette delta** |

`lawXyLinearTimeWeighted` pins it: `axisWeight X == axisWeight Y`; a flat-palette burst (`pd=0`) makes
a temporal step free of palette cost; otherwise a temporal step weighs `pd`, and for `pd>1` a t-step
costs strictly more than the same colour change in space. This is the formal home of the memory note
"t and ±1t are weighed at a per-frame color palette delta" (the GCE temporal policy/value delta).

## 6. The spine is unchanged, energy, 2×2×2→1+residuals, S/K, PonderNet

- **2×2×2 → 1 coarse + 7 residuals**, `liftOct8`/`unliftOct8`, MATCHING the shipped Zig
  `s4_octant_lift` (kernels.zig:857) byte-for-byte: nested floor pair-lifts (the S-transform),
  `sLift x y = (⌊(x+y)/2⌋, x−y)`. Coarse = the floored-MEAN lineage (so it stays in `[min,max]`,
  `lawOctantCoarseBounded`, never breaching `SUBSTRATE_BOUND`, the correction over an earlier sum-DC
  draft). Reversible because each `sLift` is the integer realisation of `[[½,½],[1,−1]] ∈ GL₂(ℤ[1/2])`
  (determinant −1, a unit); the floor makes it an exact bijection on ℤ⁸. `lawOctantLiftReversible`
  (exact integer round-trip). Energies meet the integer spine via `quantizeQ16`. The 8 octant
  children **are** the ±1 neighbourhood (`lawOctantIsNeighbourhood`): the encode-delta stencil and the
  energy-lift stencil are the same discrete-geometry object.
- **S = expand/invent (the first), K = pool/contract (the second).** `sExpand = entropy` of the coarse
  (Level 1, where invention is allowed); `kContract = residual band mass` (Level 2, the weakening).
  **S is barred on the reversible floor** (`lawSBarredOnFloor`): equal children ⟶ zero residual band ⟶
  nothing to invent (you cannot inject information into a bijection, the BCI/no-contraction guard).
  This matches locked decision #8 and `V2SkiTwoLevelEntropy`.
- **PonderNet read-depth.** `readDepth` descends rungs while the residual **band length** strictly
  decreases and halts when it bottoms, **well-founded recursion, not a Banach contraction**
  (`lawReadDepthWellFounded`, matching locked decision #7).

## 8. Open questions (V2.1-specific, to resolve before any promotion)

1. **Curve domain & resolution.** `nLevels = 256` (byte-exact). Is the curve the *full* 256-level
   PMF per channel, or a coarser binning re-expanded at collapse? (The phrase "64×64 probability
   curves" reads as 64×64 *bins each holding a curve*; if instead the curve itself is meant to be
   64-resolution, `nLevels` drops to 64 and collapse needs a re-expansion step.)
2. **Curve SHAPE for make_bins. RESOLVED (empirical histogram).** A captured bin produces integer
   counts `n(v)` over the value levels (the burst's repeated sub-observations of that voxel). The
   probability face is `p(v) = roundHalfUp(n(v)·2^16 / N)`, BYTE-IDENTICAL to the existing
   `boardMassFromCount` / `s4_board_counts_to_mass_q16` (reused, generalized to the 256-level
   alphabet). The energy face is `E(v) = N − n(v)` (`countsToEnergy`), unconditionally mode-exact:
   `collapse E = argmax n =` the most-observed value, no transcendental. The two faces are order-dual
   (`lawEnergyMassOrderDual`). REJECTED the parabola/Gaussian `E=k(v−µ)²`: it is unimodal by
   construction and cannot represent the bimodal histogram that motion/occlusion produce. Surprisal
   `−log p` and `p=exp(−E)` are the derived real-valued views for entropy/S, off the byte path.
   Gated: `Spec.V21Field.countsToEnergy`/`massFromCount` + Zig `s4_v21_counts_to_energy`.
   The histogram itself = the values of the fine samples in each box-decimation cell (the SAME box the
   cube-ladder `VoxelReduce` groups; V2.1 keeps the per-cell value distribution instead of the
   reversible Haar detail, since the pipeline is byte-exact and has no lossy pooling to histogram).
   Gated: `Spec.V21Field.accumulateHist` + Zig `s4_v21_accumulate_hist`. So
   `make_bins = s4_v21_accumulate_hist` (fine grid -> counts) `then s4_v21_counts_to_energy` (counts
   -> energy curve). The exact fine-grid layout vs the real sensor is the one device-wiring confirmation.
3. **Store curves, or store delta-of-curves?** §4 says the *encode target* is the delta; but does the
   trainer ingest absolute per-bin curves (and compute deltas itself), or pre-differenced curves?
   Affects the SIMT bin payload size (1× vs neighbour-fan).
3. **What supplies `PaletteDelta`?** Here it is an injected scalar. The real per-frame palette delta is
   a 256-colour-set distance (ties to the GCE temporal value delta), needs its own definition.
4. **Energy units / temperature.** The Boltzmann `exp(-E)` uses an implicit temperature of 1. A learned
   temperature per axis is the natural place for V2's energy-weighting (open Q D, the K3/d6 gap).
5. **Integer vs double curves. RESOLVED.** Energy lives in **Q16 integers on the spine/byte path**
   (`collapseQ16` is order-only, exact under any monotone code); the spine is the nested floor
   pair-lift (each `sLift` a unit of `GL₂(ℤ[1/2])`, matching Zig `s4_octant_lift`). The softmax /
   entropy probability view is real-valued and stays in exploration, OFF the byte-exact path. The
   gated `Spec.V21Field` carries the Q16 core.
