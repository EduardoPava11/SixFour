# Per-Capture Train+Infer That Yields Work: Hard Tasks and a Grounded Design for SixFour

## 1. The verdict

**Direction is real, but two of its four words are load-bearing and only two are supported by the literature.** "Train + infer per capture" is genuine, shipped, and reproduced. "Continuously learning" and "under a byte-exact determinism floor" are where the cited evidence stops covering you.

What the literature *does* support, strongly:

- **Training-as-the-output is a real paradigm.** TTT layers make the hidden state a small net whose per-step self-supervised gradient IS the forward pass ([Learning to (Learn at Test Time)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.04620), ICML 2025). Overfitting one tiny net to one signal and shipping its weights as the artifact is a validated codec ([COIN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03123)). Online per-frame overfitting on a ~1 ms budget is a shipped regime ([Neural Radiance Caching / instant-ngp](https://nvlabs.github.io/instant-ngp/)). The fitted per-signal code is a consumable feature, not a byproduct ([functa](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v162/dupont22a/dupont22a.pdf)).
- **A single self-supervised gradient step provably improves the real objective** when the aux and main gradients correlate ([TTT, Sun et al. 2020](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/sun20b.html)), and SixFour's byte-exact coarse-pool label makes that correlation structural, not lucky.
- **The cost is a non-issue at 21 parameters.** The scary on-device-training numbers (3.85–28 s/step, 250 MB leaks, thermal walls) are all the *LLM/Core-ML* regime ([MeBP](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03425v1); [Apple on-device updates](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/coreml/personalizing-a-model-with-on-device-updates)). A 21-param fused Metal dispatch sits 6–9 orders of magnitude below that.

What the literature does **not** support, and where the honest "partly wishful" label attaches:

- **TTT resets; it does not continuously learn.** Every TTT/functa result carries a *frozen* meta-learned `W_0` that is re-loaded per sequence and discarded after. Mapping capture→sequence gives you *within-capture* adaptation thrown away at the capture boundary. Carrying the gene forward across captures — Daniel's actual goal — is the *opposite* of what these papers do, and it reopens catastrophic forgetting / loss of plasticity that none of them study. This is the single most-repeated correction across the adversarial verdicts.
- **The determinism floor is outside all of it.** Every cited result is float SGD with non-associative GPU reductions and no quantized re-entry. Re-projecting a float step onto a Q16 integer lattice each step is unprecedented in this literature, and it is precisely where the "learning IS the output" identity breaks (sub-LSB updates round to zero; see §2).
- **"Yields work" is real but low-rate only.** COIN/functa validate weights-as-output, but at a *low bitrate*, over *thousands* of steps, on *thousands* of params. A one-dispatch 21-param fit cannot carry high-detail content; it can only carry a thin, structured residual band. Where the residual is white or below the LSB — which SixFour has already measured as its own **FLOORED** verdict — the act of learning yields *nothing*, exactly as [TTT's "airplane class" failure](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/sun20b.html) predicts.

**Net:** build it, but frame it as *amortized per-capture adaptation on a frozen meta-learned prior, gated to the residual band where the free label carries above-floor information* — not as unbounded continual acquisition. The grandiose reading ("a net that continuously learns new representation forever from one shutter press, every act of learning a shippable artifact") is where wishful thinking lives.

## 2. The hard tasks (most-binding first)

### Task 1 — The determinism-floor / trivial-target dead-zone (THE binding constraint)

**What it is.** Float gradients must re-enter an integer Q16 lattice byte-exactly. Two things collide: (a) any per-capture update whose magnitude is below one Q16 LSB rounds to *zero* — no learning; (b) the free coarse-pool label is a *deterministic reduction* of the fine cube, so on flat/low-detail captures the residual `H(fine|coarse)` is itself near-zero. When both bite, the gene sees a sub-LSB target, takes a sub-LSB step, and the lattice snaps it back to where it started.

**What the literature says.** This is SixFour's own **FLOORED / "trains but doesn't learn"** result, and the literature converges on it from three sides: TTT fails on trivial/ill-defined pretexts ([airplane class](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/sun20b.html)); the SIREN "spectral bottleneck" is a *hard collapse to near-zero output*, not slow convergence ([WINNER, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12980)); and the SSL/rate-distortion view says the codable object is exactly `H(detail|coarse)`, which is *white/zero* wherever pooling was byte-exact ([SSL-as-compression IB](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10968883/)). The multi-view IB caveat ([Wang et al.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10968883/)) is the theory-name for your floor: a coarse-only-conditioned net recovers detail no better than `I(coarse; detail)` bits, which collapses toward 0 on incompressible residual.

**How it bites SixFour specifically.** Theorem 1's guaranteed decrease is second-order-small (`~ε²/βG²`); on a tiny net with a tiny step it is *smaller than one Q16 LSB*, so the floor either rounds the gain away (no learning) or snaps a full LSB in an unguaranteed direction (loss can increase). Note the Q16 re-entry is *not* the QAS problem it superficially resembles — [Quantization-Aware Scaling](https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/file/90c56c77c6df45fc8e556a096b7a2b2e-Paper-Conference.pdf) fixes distortion introduced by an *int8 backward graph*; SixFour computes gradients in float and quantizes only at the end, so QAS is a near-no-op here. The real, unprecedented hazard is the *staircase loss* it creates for any forward-only method (§3, ZO).

### Task 2 — Representation collapse / trivial constant output

**What it is.** A per-capture learner can drive its loss to zero by emitting a constant (e.g. predict-zero-detail everywhere). A shrinking loss is not evidence of useful work.

**What the literature says.** Entropy-minimization TTA has an *exact* trivial minimizer and collapses in the wild — batch-1, mixed shift — driven by a few large-gradient samples ([SAR, ICLR 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.12400)); the gradient vanishes at both the uniform and any one-hot prediction ([Ranked Entropy Minimization, ICML 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.16441)). JEPA/BYOL defeat collapse with EMA + stop-gradient asymmetry.

**How it bites SixFour.** *Mostly dodged, but not immune.* Because the coarse-pool label is a fixed deterministic function of the real input (not a co-adapting encoder or a confidence proxy), the classic entropy-collapse minimizer *is not a minimizer of SixFour's loss* — a constant output incurs high error against varied real pools. The residue that does bite: (a) you must supervise the **residual** (fine minus lift-of-coarse), not the pooled target — a constant predictor trivially satisfies a pooled target; (b) batch-1 gives no averaging to absorb one pathological capture (blown highlights, motion blur), so a large-gradient outlier can knock a *persistent* gene into a bad basin. The correct instrument is a **diversity/effective-rank check on the gene's output**, not the loss value.

### Task 3 — Catastrophic forgetting vs loss of plasticity: the reset-vs-accumulate axis

**What it is.** If the gene persists across captures, it either forgets (over-plastic) or ossifies (loss of plasticity). Reset-per-capture is a firewall against drift but throws away the "continuous" goal.

**What the literature says.** Loss of plasticity is **near-inevitable in any forever-updating net and worst at small sizes** — dead units to ~25% (60% under Adam), unbounded weight growth, effective-rank collapse ([Dohare et al., Nature 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11338828/)). The reset/accumulate split is first-class: online-accumulate wins *only* when inputs are same/smoothly-changing ([Sun et al. 2020](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/sun20b.html)); on a non-stationary stream, accumulation is the named failure mode "error accumulation and catastrophic forgetting" ([TTA survey](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2303.15361)). The cheap fix is **Continual Backprop** — reinit the lowest-utility unit with zero *outgoing* weight ([Nature 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11338828/)); plus L2/shrink-and-perturb; **avoid Adam** (accelerates unit death).

**How it bites SixFour.** Consecutive captures are *abrupt* scene jumps (lunch → dog → sunset), exactly the regime where accumulation drifts and the online "never hurts by 0.2%" guarantee evaporates (it was measured on a *shuffled single-corruption* stream). Worse, the Q16 floor is an *accelerant* for plasticity loss, not a shield: sub-LSB gradients freeze weights permanently (the dormant-unit mechanism, arriving faster and irreversibly under a hard floor). And CBP's random reinit collides with the determinism floor unless seeded from the capture hash and re-quantized. At 21 params the population statistics (dead-unit %, effective rank) are nearly degenerate, so the *mechanism* transfers but the *instrument* is weak.

### Task 4 — Making the residual actually USEFUL (the "yields work" part)

**What it is.** The deliverable must be the residual/act of learning, not a checkpoint. But raw prediction error is dominated by irreducible noise (the "noisy-TV" problem), so it is entropy, not saliency.

**What the literature says.** Free-energy / active inference: the precision-weighted prediction *error* is the emitted signal; MDL/IB: the compressed **codable residual** `H(detail|coarse)` is the product ([SSL-as-compression](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10968883/)); EBMs: scalar energy IS the surprise readout; curiosity/RL: reward **learning progress** (Δloss), not raw error, and learn features that retain only the *predictable* part.

**How it bites SixFour.** The exact-pool label already projects out one noise axis (`ε_info = 0` for the pooled band), which is a *maximally strong* version of the IB shared-info assumption — good. But two cautions: (1) use the **Deterministic** Information Bottleneck (`H(T)`, not `I(X;T)`) and **count codable bits over the Q16 lattice** rather than estimate mutual information — MI is infinite/ill-posed for a deterministic quantized encoder, and undefined at batch-1 anyway; (2) emit the per-capture **loss-delta** (learning progress) as the saliency/work signal, not the loss. Where the residual is white, learning correctly yields nothing — degrade to the lossless lift (the "Showcase depth-0 fallback").

### Task 5 — Fitting speed / how few steps

**What it is.** One fused dispatch (or a handful of steps) must produce a useful fit.

**What the literature says.** Naive per-signal overfit is brutal — [COIN needs ~15k–50k Adam steps](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03123) and only beats JPEG after 15k. The escape is **meta-learned initialization**: [functa fits a new signal in 3 SGD steps](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v162/dupont22a/dupont22a.pdf) from a zero-init latent because an offline outer loop pre-shaped the landscape; instant-NGP moves capacity into a trainable *encoding*, not the net.

**How it bites SixFour.** The 3-step result is *purchased* by expensive offline meta-training and by fitting a small **modulation/latent through a frozen projection**, not free weights. SixFour is architecturally ahead here — its persistent offline field encoder IS the meta-init — but "one dispatch works" is *contingent* on paying that offline bill, and functa's own caveat applies: a 3-step, tiny-code fit has a **hard fidelity ceiling that worsens with scene complexity**. Do not expect COIN-level fidelity from one step; scope the gene to the low-rate band.

### Non-task: raw cost/latency/memory

Enumerated because the brief asks, but it does **not** bind. 21 params ⇒ arithmetically sub-millisecond; memory is a rounding error (no frozen backbone to stream, unlike [MeBP's 320 MB–1 GB](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.03425v1)); one dispatch per shutter press is not "sustained training," so the thermal/battery wall doesn't ramp. The *real* residual cost is **fixed per-dispatch overhead** (Metal command-buffer encode/commit round-trip, MPSGraph compile/warmup) — which none of the cost papers isolate because their numbers are swamped by weight streaming. Custom hand-written Metal training kernels are a proven production path down to iPhone 12 ([Metal FlashAttention 2.0](https://engineering.drawthings.ai/p/metal-flashattention-2-0-pushing-forward-on-device-inference-training-on-apple-silicon-fe8aac1ab23c)), and MPSGraph exposes autodiff, so the fused-dispatch bet is sound.

## 4. The design recommendation

Structure per-capture learning as **amortized, gated, residual-band adaptation on a frozen meta-learned prior**, with the residual (and its bit-count / loss-delta) as the emitted work. Concretely, tied to the existing pieces:

**Offline (Mac), pays the amortization bill:** Meta-learn the θ_up gene's init `W_0`, a per-parameter learned step size (meta-SGD), and — optionally — the view projections θ_K/θ_V/θ_Q so the gene reconstructs a *learned* view of the fine cube while the byte-exact coarse pool stays the determinism-friendly default (θ_V = pool). This is what buys the 1–3-step on-device fit; without it, one dispatch fits nothing (COIN's lesson).

**Per-capture, on device — the S/K/I / yin-yang mapping is the control structure, not decoration:**
- **K (contract / pool)** is the free anchor: the byte-exact coarse pool is `θ_V x`, the deterministic label. It costs nothing and cannot be a noisy pseudo-label — this is what lets SixFour skip the entire denoising/filtering machinery the TTA survey spends its bulk on.
- **I (reversible floor)** is the lossless lift / Showcase fallback: the gene is inserted as a **gated residual over the lift** (`tanh(α)·gene + lift`, α starting ≈0), so worst case it degrades to byte-exact lossless — the determinism-safe way to introduce a float learner.
- **S (expand / invent)** is the *only* place a gradient step is spent, and it is **gated**: run the dispatch **only where the residual clears the Q16 floor** and the gradient-correlation `⟨∇l_main, ∇l_pool⟩` is positive. Where the residual is white/sub-LSB, skip — do not burn a dispatch that cannot correlate (the airplane-class rule). This directly discharges the FLOORED failure by construction: you never ask the gene to invent where `E[detail|coarse]≈0`.

**Emit the work, not the checkpoint.** The deliverable is the residual the gene synthesizes *plus* the per-capture **loss-delta** as a learning-progress/saliency field — never the raw loss (Pathak's "reward progress" over "reward error"). Optionally surface the codable bit-count of the residual as the honest measure of how much was invented.

**Numerics.** Momentum-free SGD (Adam accelerates unit death and buys 3× memory for less accuracy than a stateless rescale). Accumulate in FP32 through a **canonical master copy**, then round deterministically to Q16 with **your own pinned rounding** (not hardware bf16). Zero-outgoing-weight for any reinit so the lattice sees an exact no-op the step it fires.

**Reset vs accumulate — ship RESET as default.** Consecutive captures are non-stationary jumps, the regime where accumulation drifts and the online guarantee fails. Expose accumulation as a knob gated behind a **same-scene detector** *plus* an anti-forgetting anchor: CBP utility-EMA reinit of the least-useful param (fractional budget across captures, ~one reset per few hundred shots) and/or CoTTA-style stochastic restoration of `k` of 21 params. Run a cheap **plasticity meter** (dead-unit fraction, mean |weight|, effective rank) and an **EMA collapse watchdog** (0.9/0.1 of the pool-loss) that snapshot-restores the gene to seed on collapse.

**Capacity honesty.** The MIT result that "classifier-only / bias-only tuning plateaus — you must train expressive weights" ([On-Device Training Under 256 KB](https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/file/90c56c77c6df45fc8e556a096b7a2b2e-Paper-Conference.pdf)) is a warning that 21 params may sit at the plateau end. Keep the gene tiny for the *floor* path, but treat "21 is enough" as a hypothesis to test (below), and be ready to grow the per-capture **code** (functa's 64–256-dim modulation) while keeping the *net* frozen — grow the encoding, not the backprop depth (instant-ngp's lesson).

## 5. Open questions / what to prototype first

1. **Prototype the amortized single-dispatch fit on REAL bursts, and measure whether Δloss clears the Q16 floor on structured vs flat captures.** This is the one experiment that discharges the FLOORED verdict end-to-end: does a meta-initialized 21-param gene, in one fused dispatch, move above one LSB on a textured capture and correctly no-op on a flat one? If it floors even with a good init on real data, the gene needs more capacity or a richer (paint/perceptual) target, not more steps.
2. **Bit-reproducibility audit of the fused Metal gradient across A-series and M-series.** Confirm the FP32-accumulate → pinned-round → Q16 path is byte-identical across device generations (bf16 emulation rounding and non-associative reductions are the named risks). If it isn't, the "byte-exact same burst → same GIF" promise breaks and every downstream determinism claim is void.
3. **Reset-vs-accumulate scene-jump detector.** What cheap signal (coarse-pool distance between consecutive captures? gradient-correlation sign?) reliably distinguishes "same scene, safe to accumulate" from "jump, must reset"? Without it, ship reset-only.
4. **Capacity ladder.** Does 21 params plateau? Sweep the gene size / add a functa-style frozen-projection modulation code and measure residual PSNR vs on-device cost — decouple the code sized for *useful work* from the code sized for *reconstruction*.
5. **Entropy-code the gene against the MAP-Elites archive prior.** COIN's explicitly-unexploited lever: a learned distribution over gene-space is a "significant" compression win and shrinks the per-capture payload. The archive is a natural prior.
6. **Is the "learned view" (θ_K/θ_V) worth it, or does the byte-exact pool stay the better default?** The learned view is more informative but reintroduces a training choice on the S-map (the only non-canonical map in the SES); test whether it beats the free pool enough to justify the offline complexity and the determinism risk.

**One honest caution to carry into all of the above:** the "learning yields useful work per capture" payoff is hard-capped at `I(coarse; detail)` bits — real and worth shipping on structured scenes, ~0 on noise/high-frequency captures. The design must *expect and gracefully absorb* the zero case (fall back to lossless), not treat every shutter press as guaranteed to produce invention. That is the difference between the grounded version of Daniel's direction and the wishful one.
