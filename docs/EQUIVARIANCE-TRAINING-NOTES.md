# σ-equivariance: optional Mac-side training upgrades

SixFour's look-NN is σ-equivariant under the OKLab chroma reflection
`σ(L,a,b) = (L,−a,−b)`. Today equivariance is enforced **structurally** by hard
block-diagonal weight masks (the 22-achromatic / 42-chromatic partition in
`Spec/Tensor.hs sigma64Mask`, forced into the L4/L5 weights in `Spec/LookNetR.hs`
and `Spec/LookNetD.hs`). This is correct and the right shipped design — the masks
make equivariance impossible to violate, and the symmetry prunes 45–55% of the
weights for free. The two ideas below do **not** replace that; they are trainer-side
options to fit the *non-zero* weights better, and were surfaced by a verified
2024–2026 literature sweep (see the tensor-math review in this session).

## 1. Soft-relaxation-then-project training (then ship exact)

> Pertigkiozoglou et al., *Improving Equivariant Networks with Relaxation*, NeurIPS 2024
> (arXiv:2406.09588); Kim et al., *Regularizing with auto-tuned equivariance*, ICML 2023
> (arXiv:2306.00356); Wang et al., *Relaxed group convolution*, ICML 2022.

The idea: during **training only**, relax the hard constraint to
`f(x) = f_e(x) + θ · W·x`, where `f_e` is the exactly-equivariant (masked) part and
`θ ≥ 0` scales an unconstrained correction. Training with `θ > 0` gives the optimizer
more freedom to escape bad basins; **at inference you set `θ = 0`**, recovering an
*exactly* σ-equivariant network. The published result is that this can find better
equivariant optima than training the hard-masked net directly.

**Why it's safe for SixFour:** the inference-time artifact is byte-for-byte the same
hard-masked, exactly-equivariant forward pass we already ship and golden-verify.
`θ` and the extra `W` live only in the MLX training graph and are dropped before
weight export — no new ship-side math, no dependency. The equivalent for the
auto-tuned-regularizer variant (Kim 2023) is to add an equivariance *penalty* (with
self-tuned strength) instead of the hard mask during training, then project/snap the
trained weights back onto the masked subspace before export.

**Status: WATCH / optional experiment.** Worth a Mac-side A/B (relaxed-then-project
vs current hard-mask) measured on the actual ~115K net; adopt only if it measurably
lowers `Spec/Loss.hs` loss on held-out captures. The verification refuted the
stronger claim that relaxation helps *most* on tiny nets, so treat the upside as
unproven-at-our-scale until measured.

## 2. EMLP basis derivation (replace hand-derived masks on the Mac side)

> Finzi et al., *A Practical Method for Constructing Equivariant Multilayer Perceptrons*,
> ICML 2021 (`github.com/mfinzi/equivariant-MLP`).

EMLP solves the equivariance constraint `ρ_out(g)·W = W·ρ_in(g)` for an arbitrary
matrix group and emits a basis of equivariant linear maps. Our σ reflection is exactly
the order-2 group generated by `diag(1,−1,−1)`, so EMLP can **auto-derive** the same
basis the `sigma64Mask` / `SigmaPairHead` masks encode by hand — useful as an
independent check that the hand-derived 22+21+21 block structure (and the 384-DOF
σ-pair head) is the complete equivariant basis, and as scaffolding if the channel
partition ever changes.

**Status: WATCH (Mac-side tooling only).** EMLP is a JAX research library — it can
**never** enter the iOS target. Its only role is Mac-side: derive/verify the basis,
then extract the weights into the hand-written Swift/Metal forward pass exactly as
today. Net change to the shipped product: zero.

## What this note explicitly does NOT propose

- No `mlx-swift`, no CoreML, no JAX, no relaxation parameter on device.
- No change to `sigma64`, `gmmTokenSigma`, the σ-pair genome, or any golden vector.
- The shipped forward pass remains exactly σ-equivariant and hand-written.
